## 1. Website Overview & Strategy

GtaLobby.com is a comprehensive GTA resource hub structured using SAG (Semantic Authority Grid) methodology. The site covers both GTA 5/Online and GTA 6 as equal first-class citizens — not as a GTA 5 site with a GTA 6 afterthought. Keyword analysis of 672 mapped keywords reveals that GTA 6 anticipation content alone generates 2.67M US monthly searches (more than double all GTA 5 content combined), with 123 of those keywords at KD ≤ 20. This is a once-in-a-generation SEO window.

| Languages | English primary, Spanish planned (73K+ global 'trucos' volume) |

### The Dual-Game Thesis

GTA 5 content provides immediate authority and establishes the site's topical foundation. GTA 6 content captures the massive pre-launch search demand at historically low keyword difficulty. When GTA 6 launches (2026–2027), the site already has indexed, ranking GTA 6 pages that transition from anticipation to definitive guides. Neither game replaces the other — GTA Online will continue for years alongside GTA 6, just as it survived from 2013 to now. The architecture treats both as permanent, parallel content pillars.

## 4. Hub Page Architecture (SAG Clusters)

Every category contains 15–25 hub pages. Each hub page is an SAG cluster targeting a head keyword, linking to 10–20 child posts that capture the long-tail. Hub pages are the structural backbone; post types are the content format within.

### Hub Page Template (Same as v1)

1. H1 with natural-sentence title targeting head keyword
2. Quick answer box (featured snippet / AI Overview target)
3. Table of contents linking to sections within hub
4. Comprehensive overview (800–1,500 words)
5. Structured data section (table, comparison, database)
6. Links to all child posts organized by sub-topic
7. FAQ section (People Also Ask target)
8. Related hubs sidebar (cross-links to sibling hubs)

## 7. Custom Fields Per Post Type

Same as v1. Hub Page fields, Database fields, Weekly Recap fields, and all standard post type fields carry over unchanged. The only addition:

### GTA 6 Anticipation Fields (applies to GTA 6 category posts)

| Field | Type | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| confidence_level | Select | Confirmed / Likely / Rumored / Speculative |
| source_url | URL | Link to original leak, trailer, or Rockstar announcement |
| source_type | Select | Official Rockstar / Trailer Analysis / Datamine / Leaker / Speculation |
| last_verified | Date | When this info was last checked against latest reveals |
| post_launch_update_needed | True/False | Flag for posts that need rewriting after GTA 6 launches |
| replaces_url | URL | If this post will replace/redirect to a post-launch version |

These fields solve the pre-launch → post-launch transition problem. Every GTA 6 anticipation article is tagged with a confidence level and flagged for post-launch updates. When GTA 6 drops, you run through every flagged post and update it with confirmed information, preserving the ranking URL.

## 19. SEO Strategy

Same as v1 with one critical addition for GTA 6 content:

### GTA 6 Content SEO Rules

1. Every GTA 6 anticipation article uses 'last updated' date prominently — Google prioritizes freshness for pre-release game queries
2. Confidence badges (Confirmed / Likely / Rumored) in every article — builds trust, reduces bounce rate
3. Source citations for every claim — link to trailer timestamps, Rockstar announcements, reputable leakers
4. Schema markup: Article schema with dateModified updated on every edit — critical for freshness signals
5. "This article will be updated when GTA 6 launches" notice on every anticipation post — sets user expectation and signals ongoing maintenance to Google
6. Internal linking: Every GTA 6 article links to the GTA 6 category hub AND to relevant GTA 5 comparison content

### Hub Page Internal Linking Rules (Same as v1)

1. Every child post links back to its parent hub in the first 100 words
2. Every hub links to all its children with descriptive anchor text
3. Hubs cross-link to 2–3 related hubs in same category
4. Category archive pages list all hubs in that category
5. Homepage features top hubs from each category
6. No orphan pages — every post has 3+ internal links

### Featured Snippet & Schema Strategy (Same as v1)

FAQ schema on hubs/guides. HowTo schema on step-by-step guides. Article schema everywhere. ItemList on rankings/databases. VideoObject when YouTube embeds present.
